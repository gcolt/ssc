ARC GUIDELINES FOR FENCE INSTALLATION 

 

 

Revision: 0                                           Date: 08/07/2024

 

 

Purpose:  In accordance with our Covenants Article 3, paragraph 3.3.3, the ARC has established guidelines for fence installation in a resident’s backyard. 

 

Installation of a fence for the backyard will require an application since the ARC is responsible for reviewing modifications that affect the exterior aesthetics of each lot (see Article 3 for details). Note that installation of a fence will require a permit from the City of Port Orange.

 

ARC Standards:

 

-   Prior to installation, submit an application to the ARC (Architectural Review Committee), receive approval and obtain a Port Orange permit prior to work starting.

 

The following styles of fencing are approved:

 

-   White vinyl privacy and/or white vinyl picket fencing

-   Aluminum fence panels and wrought iron type

 

 The following styles will not be approved: Fencing made from any type of wood (pressure treated, cedar, etc), barbed wire, chain link or composite.

 

Fencing height must be between 3 feet and 6 feet.

 

ARC Use Guidelines:

 

-   If a neighbor has a fence you may bring that side of your fence equal to the existing fencing.  The other side must be equal to the depth of your lanai plus an additional 5 feet.  

-   If no fencing is currently in place at your neighbor’s homes the maximum your fencing may be placed toward the road would be the depth of your lanai on your home plus an additional 5 feet. This would be the same on both sides of your home.  If there is an obstruction located in this area, i.e., HVAC unit, pool filter, etc., you may have that side of the fence move forward that distance plus an additional 2 feet so that maintenance may be performed on the equipment.

-   Gates may be positioned on either side of the fence.
