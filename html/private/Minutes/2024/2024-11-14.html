Meeting Minutes

CALL TO ORDER:

 

HOA President Klemm called the meeting to order at 6:30 PM, and

MINUTES: Mr. Klemm moved to waive the reading of the minutes of the October 2024 meeting. Mrs. Colt seconded, and motion carried.

 

TREASURER’S REPORT:

 

·       Status of Accounts & Financial Reports: Mrs. Colt reviewed the monthly financial statements. The balance sheet reflects current assets and liabilities as of October 31 of $541,567.07 in all accounts. Profit & Loss statement with accompanying line item notes were provided and discussed, reflecting YTD income and expenses of $218.881.32 and $209,061.63, respectively. It was agreed the fountain repairs should be taken from the Reserve Account

·       Status of assessments and legal: Of 307 accounts, 29 are in arrears with 9 owing more than $200 and 7 owing less than $200. 254 have a zero balance, and 45 have a credit balance. One of the delinquent accounts has requested time to make payments, but to date no payments have been received. All other delinquent accounts have been sent 30-day collection letters and will be referred to the Attorney for 45-day Intent to Lien notices when appropriate. There are 4 accounts that are consistently in arrears. These were identified to the Board, which concluded they should receive 45-day lien notices.

 

COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

ARC: Mr. Grear reported the committee met November 4, 2024 via Zoom. There were only two (2) applications to be considered for roof repair and replacement, and both were approved. Email was previously sent to the Board regarding annual 7 review findings that had not been corrected, and 6 remain on that list. Normal procedure is for these residents to receive a notice that the Fine committee would be meeting so they could attend, and a fine would be established. Board agreed to taking the next step for these violations.

 

Mr. Klemm noted that 2 hardwood trees had come down and had questions about whether an application for the removals had been submitted and whether the City still required there be two hardwood trees in the front of a residence. It was concluded that is no longer being strictly enforced, and permits were being waived if the trees had sustained hurricane damage.

 

GATE & ENTRY: Mr. Colt reported that the gates are in working order with no issues.

 

GROUNDS: Mrs. Pawela reported that the neighborhood is slowly getting cleaned up from the hurricane damage and resulting debris. The downed trees have been removed and the resulting holes have been back filled and leveled. The stumps from the accidentally removed palm trees have been ground down. She noted that she was not notified in advance as she had requested, so that the electric and irrigation lines could be marked to ensure they were not severed or damaged. Fortunately, everything appears to be working and no damage seems to have been done. The trees will be replaced at the tree company’s expense. They are aware we want to have a say in what trees will be planted so that we get something comparable to the ones that were mistakenly removed. Tout has communicated that pictures of the proposed trees will be provided.

 

Mrs. Paweła initiated a discussion concerning the need to get proposals in from other Landscapers as Donnie has not been performing up to the standards we expect for maintenance of the community common areas. He has been given multiple opportunities to improve and still has not performed the mowing, weeding and other tasks necessary to keep the community grounds looking as well as they should. One proposal was presented to the Board for review from Larry Butler DBA All About Outdoors, which includes letters of recommendation from a few residents of the Sanctuary. Tout has been requested to obtain additional proposals. While Mr. Butler’s proposal is acceptable, the Board would like to see at least one additional proposal before making a decision.

 

A discussion followed regarding replacing the fence along RTT with a hedge or some kind of planting which would be easily replaced should it be damaged. In any event the existing fence needs to be repaired. The Board requested Tout obtain estimates to have that done. It was agreed that we will need to maintain the existing fence for a few more years until a living fence can get established and it can be removed entirely. Mrs. Pawela will explore types of hedge plants and what, if any, upgrades would be required for the irrigation system in that area.

 

NEWSLETTER: Deadline is early November for the December issue.

NEW BUSINESS:

·       Adoption of Proposed 2025 Budget – Proposed Budget was discussed. Mr. Klemm moved to approve the 2025 budget as presented with the quarterly assessment of $215. Mrs. Colt seconded, motion carried.

·       Sable Creek /surrounding storm drain matters – Mr. Klemm provided information he obtained from the City of Port Orange attorney. The City indicated that the property at the west end of Roscoe Turner that contains the 30-inch water and sewer mains that has been subject to some erosion does not belong to them as far as they can determine, which we knew to be the case. Our attorney advised us to recontact the St. Johns Water Management District (SJWMD). Mr. Klemm spoke with the Ombudsman who has moved the issue up to the regulatory level. It is possible that the box culvert could become an asset that we will need to maintain. The question that arises is that we have never been given a permit to put our culvert in the County’s or the State’s river. The creek does not belong to us. We can traverse that waterway, but since we are in the creek with our culvert, it may be questionable. Sable Creek wants to meet and it is anticipated they will want us to maintain the box culvert. And we have asked for a determination on the outfall structure at the west end of RTT where our permit ends, and Sable Creek is stating that we need to fix that end. Sable Creek has asked for a meeting and it is anticipated they will make a demand that we fix it. We will not be doing anything in the creek. If Sable Creek were to sue us and demand the pipes be restored to the way they were in 1943, we would fight it and demand they show us where we need to maintain them. We await a determination from SJWMD.
