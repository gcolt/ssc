OBJECTIVE:

Ensure that resurfacing and staining of driveway surfaces satisfy the sprit of our CC&R’s, maintain our community’s standards of a high-quality appearance and to comply with Florida statutes for environmental management with a special emphasis for storm water runoff.

 

RESURFACE AND STAIN GUIDELINES:

1)     Resident must contract with a licensed specialty contractor and obtain city permits if required.

 

2)     Stained driveways must be solid patterns with no inserts (diamond shapes, geometric shapes, free form etc.) in all stain applications.  Staining is limited to the driveway and entryway and does not include the sidewalk nearest the road and driveway apron.  Stain colors are limited to (Sherwin Williams “Gull Gray” HC 132 ) Solvent based staining of driveways will be allowed with the following conditions:

a.     Contractors must provide a statement that warrants the work against peeling, chipping or cracking for a minimum of three years.

b.     Contractors must also ensure that all materials and processes used are environmentally friendly and meet all regulatory requirements.

 

3)     Textured concrete coatings will be allowed, with ARC approval of the color and pattern.  Colors will be limited to gray or tan (same as stains), and patterns will be limited to a continuous knock-down, or an ARC approved plain pattern. Concrete coatings will be for the driveway and entryway and do not include the sidewalk nearest the road and driveway apron.  No inserts of any kind will be allowed.

a.     Contractor must provide a statement that warrants the work against peeling, cracking or chipping for a minimum of 5 years.

b.     Contractors must haul away any excess product to an area designated for such product waste.  Nothing shall be left behind and storm sewers are not to be used for any disposal purpose.

c.     Contractor must ensure that all materials used in resurfacing are environmentally friendly and meet all regulatory requirements.
