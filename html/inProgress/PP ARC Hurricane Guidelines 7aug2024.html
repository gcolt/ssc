PINNACLE OF PORT ORANGE

HOMEOWNERS ASSOCIATION, INC.

 

 

ARC GUIDELINES FOR HURRICANE SHUTTERS

 

Revision: 0                                           Date: 08/07/2024

 

 

Purpose:  In accordance with FL HB No.293 and our Covenants Article 3, paragraph 3.3.3, the ARC has established guidelines for hurricane protection for various openings in a residence. 

 

Installation of permanent hurricane shutters will require an application since the ARC is responsible for reviewing modifications that affect the exterior aesthetics of each lot (see Article 3 for details). Note that installation of permanent hurricane shutters will require a permit from the City of Port Orange.

 

There is also cause for concern that windows covered for a hurricane event prevent egress/ingress in case of fire or other emergency. Those issues are the responsibility of each homeowner.

 

ARC Standards: 

-       Permanently mounted enclosures must match the approved color scheme for the house.

-       Prior to installation, submit an application to the ARC (Architectural Review Committee), receive approval and then obtain a Port Orange Permit prior to any work starting.

 

The following permanent style shutters are approved:

 

-       Rolling Hurricane Shutter System - Direct Mount

 

-       Accordion Hurricane Shutters – Direct Mount

 

No other shutter styles will be approved, including but not limited to Bahamas, Colonials and Clamshell.

 

 

The following temporary storm coverings do not require ARC approval: 

 

-       Clear, aluminum, fabric or plywood

 

 

 

ARC Use Guidelines:

 

-       Shutters and temporary storm coverings may only be closed or put in place when Volusia County EOC announces a hurricane event is imminent. 

 

-       All shutters and temporary storm coverings must be removed within 14 days of the hurricane event following the Volusia County all clear announcement. 

 

-       Season long installation of hurricane shutters/coverings on an entire house will not be permitted.  If the Owner will not be in residence during the hurricane season, consider contracting with a company to install/remove hurricane protection for the event. 

 

-       Partial installation of hurricane shutters/coverings on a home for the season must be approved in writing by the Board of Directors. 

 

-       No temporary storm coverings will be stored on the exterior of the residence.
